The spring session of Cornwall Council saw a busy programme of debate, scrutiny, and decision-making. CING councillors played an active role throughout, raising questions on behalf of their communities and pressing the administration on a number of important issues.

During the full council meeting, our members tabled questions on the state of road maintenance across the county, highlighting the growing backlog of repairs and the impact on rural communities in particular. The responses from the cabinet member acknowledged the scale of the challenge, and we will continue to press for a credible plan to address it in the coming budget cycle.

In the planning committee, CING councillors spoke on several significant applications, emphasising the need for genuinely affordable housing within new developments and for proper consideration of the cumulative impact of holiday-let conversions on local housing supply. We were pleased to see some of these concerns reflected in the conditions attached to approved schemes.

The scrutiny committee also examined the Council's progress on its climate action plan. Our members asked searching questions about the gap between targets and delivery, and called for more transparent reporting so that residents can judge for themselves whether sufficient progress is being made. We remain committed to constructive challenge on this and every other area of council business.
